# US Market Checklist

Use this reference when the user asks for a broader US market review or watchlist triage.

## Index Proxies

- SPY: S&P 500 large-cap risk appetite.
- QQQ: Nasdaq 100 and mega-cap technology leadership.
- IWM: Russell 2000 small-cap breadth and domestic cyclicality.
- DIA: Dow industrials and defensive/cyclical blend.
- VIX: implied volatility and hedging pressure.
- COR1M: 1-month implied correlation / correlation-risk gauge. Use it to judge whether index risk is becoming more systemic or whether dispersion and stock-picking conditions are improving.

## Sector Proxies

- XLK: technology.
- XLY: consumer discretionary.
- XLC: communication services.
- XLF: financials.
- XLE: energy.
- XLV: healthcare.
- XLI: industrials.
- XLP: consumer staples.
- XLU: utilities.

## Macro Drivers

- DXY / US dollar direction: rising dollar can pressure multinational earnings, commodities, emerging-market risk appetite, and high-duration growth stocks.
- Treasury yields: watch 2-year yield for Fed path and 10-year yield for discount-rate pressure.
- 10-year real yield / TIPS yield: important for equity multiples, gold, long-duration technology, and risk appetite.
- Breakeven inflation: compare with real yields to separate inflation repricing from real-rate tightening.
- Oil prices when inflation or energy sector leadership matters.
- Fed expectations and upcoming FOMC events.
- CPI, PCE, jobs report, retail sales, ISM, and GDP releases.

## Macro Interpretation

- DXY up + real yields up: tighter financial conditions; be cautious with high-multiple growth and speculative risk.
- DXY down + real yields stable/down: supportive for risk assets, especially growth and international revenue exposure.
- Nominal yields up but real yields flat: inflation expectation move may matter more than pure discount-rate pressure.
- Real yields down sharply: can support long-duration growth, gold, and broad equity multiples if recession risk is not rising at the same time.

## Volatility And Correlation

- VIX up + COR1M up: index-level stress is broadening; correlations are rising and diversification may work less well.
- VIX up + COR1M flat/down: volatility is rising but dispersion may remain tradable; stock selection can still matter.
- VIX down + COR1M down: healthier risk-on tape; dispersion and relative-strength trades can work better.
- COR1M rising while indexes are flat: watch for hidden crowding, index hedging demand, or narrowing leadership.

## Trading Posture Labels

- Aggressive risk-on: indexes above key moving averages, broad leadership, constructive breadth.
- Selective risk-on: leadership exists but breadth is narrow or event risk is elevated.
- Defensive: indexes below important levels, volatility rising, leadership concentrated in defensive sectors.
- Wait-and-see: major data, FOMC, earnings, or technical inflection pending.
